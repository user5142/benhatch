About

Hello friend! Thanks for stopping by and taking some time to learn more about me.

Where to begin? Well, I am mostly just a regular guy from a middle class background who has been dealt a few crappy hands, but has also had some nice luck, wins and redemption in life.

I grew up in a small town in Massachusetts, about 40 minutes east of Boston. My college friend Mark used to joke that every person he meets from Massachusetts is from a small town 40 minutes outside of Boston. I think there’s some truth to that.

When I was 14, my mom was diagnosed with cancer. Two years later, she passed away. This was a big inflection point in my life. Everything up until her diagnosis felt like the white picket fence American dream in our family. Life felt like Frodo Baggins in the Shire before he found the ring. Happy, healthy and full of adventure. And everything that came after felt like Frodo facing his long and perilous journey to Mordor.

Losing my mom was very hard on me, but I tried even harder to pretend that I was okay. I thought it was my duty as a young man to show no weakness. So I didn’t cry, didn’t talk much about it and carried on. This ultimately lead to a total mental and emotional breakdown that landed me in a psychiatric hospital on my 21st birthday. My body could no longer handle the delayed grief. My psyche could no longer bear the weight of denial.

The following year was the hardest of my life. I had to stay home and miss my senior year of college to recover. The doctors couldn’t really pinpoint what happened to me, but chalked it up as an episode of delirium. Whatever it was, it KO’d me like a Wonderboy karate kick to the soul. I sank into a deep depression. I felt isolated and alone. I had to sit on the sidelines while my friends danced across the finish line of senior year. I cycled through a number of different medications, all of which made me feel worse.

Fortunately, I was eventually able to crawl out of the pits of despair with the support of friends, family and the talented healthcare practitioners of the Greater Boston area. I returned to college a year later and finished out my degree strong. It was a long road to recovery, but I was back to feeling like myself and enjoying life again.

I wish that I could say it was smooth sailing from there, that things stayed on the up and up and I knocked down the challenges of career, romance and young adulthood like Gronk stiff-arming his way to the endzone. But alas, this was not the case. Not at all.

My twenties panned out more like the Mark Sanchez butt fumble. I landed a promising job at a growing software startup soon after graduation, only to get my heart broken by a colleague, burn out and quit a year later. I hopped from job to job, not giving myself enough of a chance to grow and leaving a trail of less than ideal resume milestones. I let my drinking habits get out of control, picked a fight on a particularly belligerent night, and got my jaw broken in two places. The attractive young Brazilian woman I had been dating at the time stopped talking to me (can’t blame her). I quit my job and decided to become a rideshare driver while pursuing freelance filmmaking. I went on to spend so much time in the car giving rides that I developed chronic back pain. The list goes on and on. You get the gist.

Although I have many regrets from these young adult years, as mentioned before, my story is not all doom, gloom and foibles. Lady luck, and perhaps a few guardian angels, have had my back many times over. For example, the double fracture of my jaw turned out to be a clean break and not displaced, and therefore did not require any surgery. I was back to work in less than a week, and the fractures healed naturally. I have had many such lucky breaks (pun intended) throughout my life.

More important than the favors of chance, I have been able to, slowly but surely, learn the important lessons behind my missteps. As I finally began to put the pieces together in my late twenties and transitioned into my thirties, things started to click. I cut out the bad habits that had been sabotaging my potential, including weed and alcohol. I found a steady job that I could settle into and build upon. I prioritized relationships and set a goal to find a girlfriend. I met this goal and found the love of my life in the middle of a pandemic. One year after meeting, we got married. A year after that, we had our first kid. Through it all, I focused on relentless improvement of my finances, fitness and career development. Mistakes and missteps were still made, but I now had the fortified foundation in place to bounce back from them quickly and course correct.

Things have gotten much better for me, but I don’t have it all figured out by any means. I am continually striving to learn and improve, while making plenty more mistakes along the way. Through this website and other channels, I hope to share some of my hard earned wisdom with others. I have found it quite fun to organize my thoughts into writing, transmit them across hundreds of miles of cables, routers, switches and servers, and deliver them to a few people on the internet who might find them helpful or entertaining.

To learn more about what I am up to these days, check out my now page. To stay up to date on new stuff, subscribe to my email list below. Have a wonderful day!
