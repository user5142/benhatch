3 Tips to Maximize the Codecademy Experience

Ben Hatch

May 7, 2023

Back in December of 2022, I embarked on Codecademy’s Full-Stack Engineer Path.

The course teaches the necessary skills to build web applications from start to finish and launch a career as a Full-Stack Software Engineer.

As of this writing, I am one-third of the way through the course and have enjoyed it more than I could have imagined. Learning about the foundational technologies of the web has been fascinating, and I love being able to learn at my own pace through the online, asynchronous format.

Platforms like Codecademy are well suited for self-starters. You get what you put into it. Staying motivated and utilizing the many resources at your disposal will help you get the most value out of the subscription.

I still have a ways to go towards completing the Full-Stack Career Path, but have picked up some helpful learning strategies along the way.

Read on for 3 tips to maximize the Codecademy experience and get the most value out of your online coding education.

1) Build a knowledge repository

One limitation of Codecademy I have found is that it is fairly difficult to return to previous modules and lessons to review material.

A key part of learning to code is revisiting lessons and documentation over and over to help solve problems and refresh your knowledge.

To solve this limitation, I created my own “KnowledgeHub” repository to store all Codecademy lessons as I complete them. Whenever I need to review material from a prior module, I can simply flip through my KnowledgeHub folders within VSCode for quick and easy access to my previous work.

2) Frequent the forums

I have been a part of several online forum communities over the years. The Codecademy forums are among the most positive and supportive I have come across.

Utilizing the Codecademy forums is very helpful for solving coding problems, connecting with other developers and getting feedback on your work.

The forums are also a great place to hone your coding skills. Other Codecademy learners consistently post coding problems they are struggling with, which is a great opportunity to take a crack at solving them and help out fellow students.

3) Polish portfolio projects

The thing I most look forward to within the Full-Stack Engineer Path are the portfolio projects. They present an opportunity to utilize all of your knowledge and skills to build a web project infused with your unique creativity and perspective.

It can be tempting to blaze through the portfolio projects so that you can keep moving that progress bar along and work towards completion of a course. I find that resisting this urge and spending the extra time and effort on each portfolio project pays big dividends.

Ultimately, the portfolio projects you complete teach you the most and become part of your developer resume. They can be prominently displayed on a personal website, and can go a long way towards helping you land a full-time job or freelance work.

I hope these simple tips can help other Codecademy learners out there maximize their online education. Feel free to reach out if you have any questions or helpful tips of your own to share!
